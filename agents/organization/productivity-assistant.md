## Task Management Systems

### Getting Things Done (GTD)

#### The Five Steps
1. **Capture**: Collect everything that has your attention
2. **Clarify**: Process what each item means and what to do about it
3. **Organize**: Put items where they belong
4. **Reflect**: Review and update your lists regularly
5. **Engage**: Do the work

#### MoSCoW Method
- **Must Have**: Critical, non-negotiable
- **Should Have**: Important but not critical
- **Could Have**: Nice to have
- **Won't Have**: Not now (defer to future)

## Focus & Deep Work Strategies

### Pomodoro Technique
```
25 min - Focus (1 Pomodoro)
5 min - Break
25 min - Focus (2 Pomodoros)
5 min - Break
25 min - Focus (3 Pomodoros)
5 min - Break
25 min - Focus (4 Pomodoros)
20-30 min - Long Break
```

### Deep Work Rules
1. **No Distractions**: Phone off, apps closed, door closed
2. **Single Task**: Work on ONE thing at a time
3. **Time-Boxed**: Set specific start and end times
4. **Clear Goal**: Know what you want to accomplish
5. **Track Progress**: Note what you complete

### Focus Enhancement
- **Environment**: Dedicated workspace, minimal clutter
- **Tools**: Noise-canceling headphones, focus music
- **Apps**: Forest, Freedom, Cold Turkey (block distractions)
- **Physical**: Proper lighting, comfortable setup
- **Mental**: Meditation, mindfulness practice

## Productivity Techniques

### Eat the Frog
Do your hardest, most important task first thing in the morning when you have the most energy.

### 2-Minute Rule
If a task takes less than 2 minutes, do it immediately instead of adding it to your list.

### Batching
Group similar tasks together:
- Email processing (3x per day)
- Phone calls (Tuesday afternoon)
- Content creation (Wednesday morning)
- Meetings (Tuesday/Thursday)

### Time Blocking
Assign specific time blocks to specific activities:
```
9:00-11:00   Deep Work Block
11:00-11:30  Email & Messages
11:30-12:00  Quick Tasks
12:00-1:00   Lunch
1:00-2:00    Meetings
2:00-4:00    Deep Work Block
4:00-5:00    Admin & Planning
```

### Timeboxing
Set a fixed time limit for tasks:
- "I'll spend exactly 1 hour on this presentation"
- "30 minutes to clean up my inbox"
- Prevents perfectionism and analysis paralysis

## Overcoming Procrastination

### Why We Procrastinate
- Task is too big/overwhelming
- Don't know where to start
- Fear of failure
- Perfectionism
- Task is boring/unpleasant

### Anti-Procrastination Strategies
1. **Break it down**: Smaller tasks are less intimidating
2. **Start tiny**: Just 5 minutes
3. **Remove friction**: Make it easy to start
4. **Add friction**: Make distractions harder
5. **Accountability**: Tell someone or public commitment
6. **Reward yourself**: Celebrate progress
7. **Just start**: Action creates motivation
