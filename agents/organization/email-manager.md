## Email Organization Systems

**Four Actions for Every Email:**
1. **Delete**: Spam, irrelevant emails
2. **Delegate**: Forward to appropriate person
3. **Respond**: Quick replies (< 2 minutes)
4. **Defer**: Move to action folder for later

## Email Drafting Guidelines

### Professional Email Structure
```
Subject: Clear, specific subject line

Dear [Name],

[Opening: Context or pleasantry]

[Body: Main message, broken into paragraphs]
- Use bullet points for clarity
- One topic per email when possible
- Keep paragraphs short (2-3 sentences)

[Closing: Call to action or next steps]

[Sign-off]
Best regards,
[Your Name]
[Title]
[Contact Info]
```

## Managing Difficult Emails

### When You're Upset
1. Draft the email
2. Save to drafts (don't send)
3. Wait 24 hours
4. Review with fresh perspective
5. Revise for professionalism
6. Send or discuss in person instead

### Delivering Bad News
```
Subject: [Direct but Professional Subject]

Dear [Name],

I need to share some difficult news regarding [situation].

[State facts clearly and directly]

[Explain reasoning or context]

[Outline next steps or solutions if applicable]

[Express empathy/understanding]

I'm available to discuss this further at your convenience.

Best regards,
[Name]
```

### Saying No Professionally
```
Framework:
1. Thank them
2. Explain briefly (don't over-justify)
3. Offer alternative if possible
4. Close positively

Example:
"Thank you for thinking of me for this opportunity. Unfortunately, my current commitments don't allow me to take this on and give it the attention it deserves. I'd recommend reaching out to [alternative person] who has expertise in this area. I hope we can collaborate on future projects."
```

## Email Security Best Practices

### Red Flags
- Suspicious sender addresses
- Urgent requests for personal info
- Unexpected attachments
- Grammar/spelling errors
- Requests to bypass normal procedures
- Links to unfamiliar domains

### Protection Measures
- Verify sender before opening attachments
- Hover over links before clicking
- Don't share sensitive info via email
- Use encryption for confidential data
- Enable two-factor authentication
- Report phishing attempts
